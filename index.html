SherriBerryApps

After attending a 12-week coding boot camp in 2015, I began developing a couple of apps. One is a travel app that makes it easy for people to share their road trip with friends and family. Another is an easy and fun budget and money-management system for creatives.

The journey is endless. There are so many exciting software tools that I'm learning, such as Material Design, MongoDB, Node.js, Meteor, AngularJS and Ionic, while continuing to practice the basics - JavaScript, HTML and CSS. I'm also working on completing FreeCodeCamp's coding challenges. FreeCodeCamp is a great place to learn how to code. When you finish the self-paced challenges, you do real coding for nonprofits.

Journalism

I've been a journalist for more than 18 years. Most recently, I was the editor for the Orange County Register's university sections. Before that, I was a technology reporter for the Minneapolis Star Tribune, and I spent many happy years writing for Orange County Business Journal. My first job as a journalist was at the Las Vegas Business Press.

Mind you, I don't have a journalism degree. I have a bachelor's in Hotel Administration. Here's a secret: a journalism degree is not needed. An extreme work ethic is needed, though. I've written for local, regional and national magazines as a freelancer. I've also written for online publications, including most recently CMSWire.

Pig Popular

Heads up! Pig Popular, the surfing and skateboarding pig is finally on his way to publication. Buy it here "Pig Popular: Some Days are Gnarly, Others are Barley" is a children's chapter book, suitable for children in grades first to fifth; ages 5 to 10. Similar books include "Winnie-the-Pooh," and the Geronimo Stilton and Junie B. Jones series.

I'm also about to publish a more gloomy set of flash fiction, and I'm working on a young-adult novel that's set in Las Vegas. Inspired by Arthur Conan Doyle's Sherlock Holmes, "Marlowe" is about a 22-year-old reporter who is working on a story about a number of mysterious deaths that take place in Las Vegas casinos.

I've recently been inspired by Andy Weir's The Egg. There are also at least two short video adaptations that I really liked here and here.
